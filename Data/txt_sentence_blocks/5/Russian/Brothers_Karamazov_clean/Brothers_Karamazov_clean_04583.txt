He was like a man speaking in a circle of intimate and sympathetic friends . 
His voice was a fine one , sonorous and sympathetic , and there was something genuine and simple in the very sound of it . 
But everyone realised at once that the speaker might suddenly rise to genuine pathos and `` pierce the heart with untold power . '' 
His language was perhaps more irregular than Ippolit Kirillovitch 's , but he spoke without long phrases , and indeed , with more precision . 
One thing did not please the ladies : he kept bending forward , especially at the beginning of his speech , not exactly bowing , but as though he were about to dart at his listeners , bending his long spine in half , as though there were a spring in the middle that enabled him to bend almost at right angles .
