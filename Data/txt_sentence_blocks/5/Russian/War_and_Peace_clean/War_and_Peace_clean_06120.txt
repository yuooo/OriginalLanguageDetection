She did not understand why he stepped out from the window to the veranda and smiled under his mustache and winked so joyfully , when warm steady rain began to fall on the dry and thirsty shoots of the young oats , or why when the wind carried away a threatening cloud during the hay harvest he would return from the barn , flushed , sunburned , and perspiring , with a smell of wormwood and gentian in his hair and , gleefully rubbing his hands , would say : `` Well , one more day and my grain and the peasants ' will all be under cover . '' 
Still less did she understand why he , kindhearted and always ready to anticipate her wishes , should become almost desperate when she brought him a petition from some peasant men or women who had appealed to her to be excused some work ; why he , that kind Nicholas , should obstinately refuse her , angrily asking her not to interfere in what was not her business . 
She felt he had a world apart , which he loved passionately and which had laws she had not fathomed . 
Sometimes when , trying to understand him , she spoke of the good work he was doing for his serfs , he would be vexed and reply : `` Not in the least ; it never entered my head and I would n't do that for their good ! 
That 's all poetry and old wives ' talk -- all that doing good to one 's neighbor !
