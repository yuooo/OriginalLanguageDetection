Pierre had none of the practical persistence that would have enabled him to attend to the business himself and so he disliked it and only tried to pretend to the steward that he was attending to it . 
The steward for his part tried to pretend to the count that he considered these consultations very valuable for the proprietor and troublesome to himself . 
In Kiev Pierre found some people he knew , and strangers hastened to make his acquaintance and joyfully welcomed the rich newcomer , the largest landowner of the province . 
Temptations to Pierre 's greatest weakness -- the one to which he had confessed when admitted to the Lodge -- were so strong that he could not resist them . 
Again whole days , weeks , and months of his life passed in as great a rush and were as much occupied with evening parties , dinners , lunches , and balls , giving him no time for reflection , as in Petersburg .
