Madame Schoss , who had been out to visit her daughter , increased the countess ' fears still more by telling what she had seen at a spirit dealer 's in Myasnitski Street . 
When returning by that street she had been unable to pass because of a drunken crowd rioting in front of the shop . 
She had taken a cab and driven home by a side street and the cabman had told her that the people were breaking open the barrels at the drink store , having received orders to do so . 
After dinner the whole Rostov household set to work with enthusiastic haste packing their belongings and preparing for their departure . 
The old count , suddenly setting to work , kept passing from the yard to the house and back again , shouting confused instructions to the hurrying people , and flurrying them still more .
