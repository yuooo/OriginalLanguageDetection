Tholomyes replied to none of them . 
Fantine heard the gossips say , as they looked at her child : `` Who takes those children seriously ! 
One only shrugs one 's shoulders over such children ! '' 
Then she thought of Tholomyes , who had shrugged his shoulders over his child , and who did not take that innocent being seriously ; and her heart grew gloomy toward that man . 
But what was she to do ? 
She no longer knew to whom to apply . 
She had committed a fault , but the foundation of her nature , as will be remembered , was modesty and virtue . 
She was vaguely conscious that she was on the verge of falling into distress , and of gliding into a worse state . 
Courage was necessary ; she possessed it , and held herself firm . 
The idea of returning to her native town of M. sur M. occurred to her .
